Reporting Bias

This page provides the details underpinning an example of reporting bias. This example focuses on one clinical recommendation, topical NSAIDs versus placebo for non-back pain over 1-7 days, from a larger, very well-done clinical practice guideline (PMID 32805126). The goal of this example is to demonstrate the need for authors of guidelines and evidence syntheses to provide rates that describe the possibility of publication bias their underlying evidence base:
  • Registration rate of published subjects (pReg/Pub)
    • Prospective registration rate of published subjects (Reg/Pub)
  • Publication rate of registered subjects (Pub/Reg)
This example has been presented at the 2022 Annual Meeting of the Society of General Internal Medicine (see Notes below).

Contents

  • Registration rate (Rate Reg/Pub) of published subjects
  • Publication rate (Rate Pub/Reg) of subjects registered at ClinicalTrials.gov
  • Excluded by APC from pairwise analysis but may be helpful

Publication rate of subjects registered at ClinicalTrials.gov

Summary

Numerator: total published and registered in 2 studies:
  • 314
Denominator: total registered in 13 studies:
  • 3474 = 2889 + 271 + 314
Publication rate of subjects registered at ClinicalTrials.gov:
  • Rate Pub/Reg: 9% (314/3474 subjects)

The CPG found 10 trials registered at ClinicalTrials.gov that were relevant but not published at the time of the ACP review (ACP Appendix 2)

Subjects: 2889 (3202 [per studies in ACP Appendix 2] - 313 in GlaxoSmithKline)
Appendix 2 lists 11 trials, but this includes one trial that posted results after the ACP search that indicate the study design is not relevant to our question:
      1. GlaxoSmithKline. Voltaren Emulgel 2% Acute Ankle Sprain Non-Inferiority Study. NCT04052620: Registration 2019 - Results 2022: 313 subjects
The 10 relevant trials that remain include a trial that posted results 06/2020 after the ACP search of 12/2019:
      2. Lau H. Safety and Efficacy of Generic Diclofenac Epolamine Acute Pain Due to Minor Ankle Sprain. PMID: NA (not published). NCT02324270: Registration 11/2014 (study started 05/2014); Results 06/2020: 647 subjects. Results showed no benefit.
Thus, 9 of the 10 relevant, unpublished trials that the the ACP indentifed at ClinicalTrials.gov are unaccounted for as of 09/04/2022. You can check the current status of these 9 studies.

We found one additional registration with results posted but has not been published

Note:
  • The 314 subjects were in the 2 trials (8% of registered subjects) that were published showed benefit of NSAIDs (Predel, NCT00955513; Predel, NCT01335724).
  • Two trials that were registered but not published posted Results showing no benefit at ClinicalTrials.gov (Lau, 2014 NCT02324270; Hamelsky, 2010 NCT00573768)
  • The eleven registered trials without results were registered by industry between the years 2007 and 2019 (median year of registration 2009).
